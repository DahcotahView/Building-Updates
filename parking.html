Parking Permits

Every resident has been issued a parking permit — and it's essential. A permit is required to park in the resident surface lot as well as in the garage.

  • Orange permits are for surface lot parking only.
  • Green permits are only for residents who have a space in the garage.

You are required to park in your designated, coordinating area overnight. This maximizes space for your fellow residents in the most courteous way — don't be the reason your neighbor can't find a parking space!

One car per leaseholder. Going forward, we can only allow one vehicle per leaseholder/resident. For any additional cars you own, we sincerely apologize — we simply don't have the space in our lots. Please look into another place to store vehicles you don't primarily use.

Need a new permit? Replacement parking permits can be purchased at the office for $100.

Guest Parking

We're happy to offer guest parking! It's located in the center back row of the parking lot, facing Cliff Road, and marked by white signs that read "Guest Parking."

  • Guest cars may park a maximum of two nights per week in the lot.
  • Guest parking is heavily monitored.
  • Residents are not allowed to park in guest parking — though you're welcome to use the rest of the entire lot.
This one matters: residents parked in guest parking WILL be towed. Anywhere else in the lot overnight is okay for orange-permitted cars.

Guests Staying Longer Than 2 Nights

No problem — they'll just need a visitor pass. Let us know through this website or by email (preferred), give us a call and leave a message, or even swing by the office so we can get everything worked out smoothly. We'll ask for the dates your guest will be staying.

On the day your guest is reported to leave, we'll ask for the visitor pass back. If the pass is held onto past the promised date and not returned, the car will be towed out of the lot — so please keep us in the loop if plans change.

New Car? Sold a Car? Rental?

Life happens and vehicles change — just keep us updated so your permit protects the right car.

  • New car: the office must verify that the registration of the added vehicle is in a leaseholder/resident's name, and that it abides by the one-car-per-leaseholder policy.
  • Sold a vehicle or using a rental: as a resident, you are responsible for transferring your parking permit to the car you're actively using to avoid tow risk.
  • Misplaced your permit in the process? A new one can be purchased in the office for $100.
About lost permits: when you purchase a replacement, the lost permit's number is reported to the tow company to make sure it can't be misused. If they spot the "lost" permit displayed on a car in our lot, that vehicle will be towed. Please be truthful with management and staff — we truly are here to help.
